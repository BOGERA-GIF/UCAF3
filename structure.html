    Structure

    The General Assembly

    The General Assembly is the highest authority of the UCAF and has power to decide upon any matters arising within UCAF.
    The General Assembly is held either as an Annual General Assembly or as an Extraordinary General Assembly. The General Assembly may be held in person, by teleconference or by another means of communication.

    Annual General Assembly
    This is held annually at such a time and place as the UCAF Executive Committee may determine. The Secretary-General is mandated to send a Circular inviting all members, together with the agenda, relevant reports, duly submitted motions, and applications at least one (1) month prior to the date of the Annual General Assembly.

    2024 UCAF Annual General Assembly

    Extraordinary General Assembly


    An Extraordinary General Assembly is convened by either a decision of the Executive Committee or following a request in writing submitted to the Secretary-General by at least a third of member Clubs. The Secretary-General is mandated to fix the date and venue for the Extraordinary General Assembly, in consultation with the President, and or the Executive Committee.

    Note.

    The Executive Committee consists of the President, three (3) Vice Presidents, Secretary General, Treasurer, and three (3) Representatives.
    Members of the Executive Committee are elected by the General Assembly to a four (4) year term, eligible for re-election..

    Judges Networks


    The UCAF Judges Network is an independent body charged with ensuring the observance of the ICF rules at UCAF-patronised competitions. The UCAF Judges Network is appointed by the UCAF Executive Committee, upon nominations from Member Clubs.
